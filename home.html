Dried nuts, such as almonds, cashews, and walnuts, undergo a dehydration process that removes their moisture content, intensifying their flavor and extending their shelf life. DRY FRUITS

Dry fruits, also known as dried fruits, are nutrient-dense snacks made by removing the water content from fresh fruits through drying methods such as sun drying or dehydration. They offer a concentrated source of vitamins, minerals, fiber, and antioxidants, making them a healthy addition to one's diet. Dry fruits come in various forms including raisins, dates, figs, apricots, and almonds, providing an array of flavors and nutritional benefits. They are often consumed as convenient snacks or incorporated into recipes to add natural sweetness and texture.
